Site Name Commentary
Kinetic

Kinetic is a web-application company that specializes in modern approaches to websites. From the UI and UX to the back-end PHP, they handle it all. Their site itself is nice, although some of their works are better in my oppinion. They are also working on adding accessibility to their sites, including this one (which my friend Gabriel is actually doing).

Fabio Azevedo's Bio

This is an example of the extreme UI that one can go for, and one of my favorite examples of what is possible with development tools. A pure work of art, the page has the fluidity of an organism and on its own justifies the author's everpresent egoism. While this, of course, would not be a great design for a commerce site or blog or such, it works great at being a portfolio -- the load time matters less due to the audience being targeted individuals, and the examples all have a pseudo-structure to them that brings everything together much unlike this paragraph.

Awwwards

While a mediocre site on its own (well, actually not too bad), this page has several examples of parrallax sites. While parallax does not by itself make a great site and can be distracting in the wrong area, most of these examples utilize good web-design techniques that create artistic and gorgeous sites. Though some suffer their usability or UX from this as well as their adaptability, for the most part these are great sites to derive inspiration and observe the potential of good site design.

Amazon

Amazon, the premiere eCommerce site of the day, utilizes several UI and UZ techniques to engage the user and create a user interaction not unlike window-shopping a store street. It also utilizes a responsive web design to make-way for the large range of devices that may utilize its services.

TV Tropes

TV Tropes is essentially an informal wiki focused primarily on media tropes from videogames, movies, animes, literature, etc. TV Tropes uses a similar design and standard for most of its pages, serving as a great example of a community-driven wiki. You can always expect the image to be in one place, the page quote at the top, etc. It is also responsive, maintaing different forms on mobile and desktop
